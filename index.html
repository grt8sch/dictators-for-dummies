Stanley Milgram's Experiments

  • Shock Experiment
    • This is Stanley Milgram’s most famous experiment. A teacher (the person being experimented on) would shock the learner everytime they got a question wrong and increase the shock level more. The learner was an actor and would intentionally get the wrong answer to get a fake shock. If the teacher did not continue, they would be told to continue. Most teachers would shock and increase the level until the learner would get a shock so strong that it could have killed them. This shows that people will be willing to kill someone if they are told by authority.
  • Conformity Experiment
    • The conformity was a social experiment where people would answer which line was longer than the others. Actors would pretend to intentionally guess the same wrong answer. The goal of the experiment was to see if the person being tested would follow along with what everyone else says or to say the correct answer.The experiment shows that people will follow others, even if they know it’s wrong.
  • Chain Letter Experiment
    • The letter experiment involved people sending letters starting a chainmail. The goal was to try to get the letter to end up to a random person. If the person mailing it did not know who the person was to send it to, they would have to send it to a contact that might know who to send it to.
